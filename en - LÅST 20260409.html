I work with turnaround and transformation leadership in technically complex businesses, where value is created at the intersection of technical choices, commercial consequences and the ability to translate decisions into robust execution in practice.

I am often brought in when organisations are technically strong but commercially challenged – or when they have high commercial ambitions without sufficient technical anchoring. In both cases, a gap emerges between the technical and the business domains, where decisions are made without full visibility of their implications and execution struggles to hold together.

My mandates typically arise in situations characterised by significant change or the need for rapid progress. This may involve turnaround situations and major transformations, industrialisation and scale-up contexts, or the build-up of new organisational structures, capabilities or delivery capacity to realise a strategic ambition. Across these situations, complexity is high, trade-offs are real and the consequences of decisions are tangible.

I work in mandate-driven roles, often with direct anchoring at CEO level and, where relevant, the board. Assignments are typically structured as cross-functional deliveries rather than embedded line roles. This enables focus on critical interdependencies across technology, economics and business priorities, and on overall progress rather than local optimisation.

Typical situations and areas of work include:

  • Turnaround and stabilisation in technically complex organisations
  • Building new organisational structures and cross-functional capabilities
  • Transformations where strategy, technology and business must work together in practice
  • Industrialisation and scale-up with operational robustness from the outset
  • Governance, decision structures and execution capacity in multi-stakeholder environments

My focus is execution under realistic conditions. This involves establishing clarity in roles, accountability and decision pathways, and ensuring alignment between technical, economic and commercial perspectives in day-to-day operations. I work hands-on with the management teams responsible for translating ambition and decisions into behaviour, capability and results.

My experience spans both industrial environments and software and technology businesses, where complexity manifests in different ways but the common denominator is the need for clear leadership, structure and discipline in execution – or building something new that will endure.
